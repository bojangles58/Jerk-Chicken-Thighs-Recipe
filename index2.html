Jerk Chicken Thighs Delight

A Traditional West Indian Dish, created with my own unique blend of spices and delectable mouth watering ingredients. Chia seeds are a great source of omega 3 and are rich in iron, magnesium and calcium. They are also gluten free.

ingredients:

  • Jerk Chicken Seasoning
  • Dried Corriander
  • Black Pepper Corn or (Ground Black Pepper)
  • Cumin or (Cumin Seeds)
  • Garam
  • Chicken Seasoning
  • Dried Mixed Herbs
  • Cajun
  • Paprika
  • Turmeric
  • Chia Seeds (a good source of calcium)
  • Chicken Oxo Cube (1)
  • Mixed Peppers (yellow, green, orange, red).
  • Onions
  • Garlic Cloves (4)
  • West Indian Hot Pepper Sauce
  • Lea and Perrins Sauce
  • Extra Virgin Olive Oil
  • Chicken Thighs (portion 6)
  • Chopped tin tomatoes.

preparation:

Step 1
  1. Pre-heat oven at 170 degrees (Gas mark 5).
  2. Add extra virgin olive oil to a roasting tray or (oven proof dish) and put tray or dish in oven.
  3. Remove skin from chicken thighs.
  4. Wash chicken thighs in a bowl with cold water and 1 or 2 lemons squeezed in or some (vinegar).
  5. Drain, then rinse off the chicken thighs under a cold tap.
  6. Drain off the excess water from the bowl containing the chicken thighs.
  7. Sprinkle 3 teaspoons of each spice above, over the chicken thighs.
  8. Dice the onions, mixed peppers and garlic cloves and add these to the chicken thighs in the bowl.
  9. Add 2 table spoons of Lea and Perrins sauce.
  10. Add 2 teaspoons of West Indian Hot Pepper Sauce.
  11. Using your hands, mix/rub in all the ingredients into the chicken thighs, making sure all the thighs are generously covered.
  12. Leave to marinate for 10 minutes or (overnight if you so wish).
  13. Carefully, using oven gloves, remove the roasting tray or oven proof dish from the oven and place on a heat resistent surface.
  14. Carefully place the chicken thighs into the roasting tray using a fork or any other suitable utensil, (even your fingers).

    15. NB: Remember, the oil will be quite hot at this point and could splatter while you are doing this.

  15. Using oven gloves, put the roasting tray with the chicken thighs into the oven.
  16. Leave to roast for around 40 mins (electric oven), or 45 mins (gas oven).
  17. After 20-25 minutes, using oven gloves, remove roasting tray from the oven and place on a heat resistent surface.
  18. Using a table spoon, carefully, collect some of the hot oil from the roasting tray and drizzle it over each chicken thigh.
  19. Return the roasting tray to the oven for the duration.
Step 2
  1. Using a suitably sized frying pan, add two table spoons of extra virgin olive oil.
  2. Slowly heat the exra virgin olive oil in the frying pan on the cooker for 2-3 minutes.
  3. Add all the seasoning ingredients (used earlier to marinate the chicken thighs), from the bowl, to the heated extra virgin olive oil in the frying pan.
  4. Stir this into the extra virgin olive oil in the frying pan and cover with a suitable lid and leave to cook for 10-15 minutes.
  5. After about 5 minutes remove the lid from the frying pan and add two-thirds of chopped tin tomatoes into the frying pan and carefully mix in with other ingredients.
  6. Return the lid to the frying pan.
  7. Remove the chicken oxo cube from its wrapper and put the oxo cube in a mug or measuring jug.
  8. Boil some water in the kettle and add enough hot water (to make the gravy), to the chicken oxo cube and stir until the chicken oxo cube has dissolved.
  9. Remove the lid from the frying pan.
  10. Pour the chicken oxo cube slowly into the frying pan and stir in.
  11. Cover the frying pan with a suitable lid and leave to simmer for 2 minutes.
  12. After 2 minutes, remove the lid from the frying pan and sprinkle in 2 table spoons of chicken gravy granuales into the frying pan and stir in.
  13. Leave to thicken for a further 2 mins.
  14. After the chicken thighs are cooked, remove the roasting tray from the oven, using oven gloves and lay the tray on a heat resistent surface.
  15. Test if properly cooked, (by using a fork). Insert the fork into chicken thighs to see if clear juices run from the chicken thighs.
  16. If happy that the chicken thighs are properly cooked, remove the lid from the frying pan.
  17. Add the chicken thighs using a spoon, to the gravy in the frying pan.
  18. Leave to cook for a further 5 minutes in order to soak in the aromas and flavours from the gravy.
  19. Using a separate clean spoon, dip into the gravy, cool the contents on the spoon by blowing slowly and taste.
  20. If happy with the flavours and consistency of the gravy then put the spoon in the washing up bowl and remove the frying pan from the heat.
  21. Otherwise, if the gravy is too thick, then water down slightly by adding boiled water from the kettle to the frying pan and stir in.
  22. Leave the contents of the pan to cool for 5 minutes before serving.
  23. Bon appetit!
